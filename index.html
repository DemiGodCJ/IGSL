God’s Provision: A Journey of Faith and Service

My Identity

My name is Charles Joshua Chakma, and I was born and raised in Chandraghona, Chittagong, Bangladesh. Growing up in a Christian family in a predominantly Muslim country, I faced many challenges. In Bangladesh, Christianity makes up only about 0.4% of the population, and as a child, I mixed with people of different faiths in educational institutions and my neighbourhood. Despite these challenges, my family's dedication to ministry has greatly influenced my faith and inspired me to deepen my relationship with Christ.
I have completed my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, which has equipped me with valuable technical skills. However, my true passion lies in serving God and sharing His message. Growing up with a father who is a pastor and a mother who is a devoted woman leader has played an important role in shaping the person I am today.

My Testimony: How I Accepted Christ

From a young age, I followed Christian traditions faithfully, and my parents were perfect role models. However, as I grew older and encountered people from different backgrounds, I slowly drifted away from the Lord. My devotion became more about routine and pride, wanting to impress others with my biblical knowledge, rather than a genuine relationship with Christ. I was deeply proud of my faith and saw it as something that made me better than others. But the true transformation came when God opened my eyes to the reality that my relationship with Him was missing the depth of love and humility. It was in that moment that I truly understood the gospel, and my heart broke as I realized I had been living for myself instead of for God. I repented and surrendered my life to Him, seeking not only His forgiveness but also His guidance in becoming a faithful follower. I accepted Jesus as my Lord and Savior and was baptized in 2020 at the age of 20. My journey of faith continues, and I strive to grow in humility, desiring a deeper relationship with God and to live according to His will.

My Vision for The Kingdom

My vision is to become a passionate servant of Christ, growing in both my knowledge and character. I desire to be a leader who leads by example, pointing people to Christ through my actions and words. I want to be someone who loves deeply, listens with compassion, and shares the gospel with humility. Through continual growth in both knowledge and character, I aim to guide others to a deeper understanding of God’s message and their purpose in His Kingdom.
As I live in a modern age, I also dream of using my technical skills to connect with people through social media and online platforms. Since many in Bangladesh primarily understand Bangla, I want to create a social media platform that provides easy Bangla translations. My future goal is to build a dedicated team that will work alongside me to translate foreign-language sermons, lectures, and gospel messages into Bangla, ensuring that everyone can access and understand the truth of God’s Word in their heart language.
This platform will serve as a lifeline for those who do not have a church nearby or cannot attend one for various reasons. Additionally, many Muslims converts in Bangladesh face persecution and cannot openly proclaim their faith in Christ without severe consequences. For them, this platform will provide a safe space where they can attend an online church, receive spiritual nourishment, and grow in their faith without fear.
I believe that by living with grace and unwavering faith, I can help others experience the transformative power of God’s love, just as I have. My heart’s desire is to equip others to walk in the fullness of their faith, whether they are new to Christianity or have been walking with the Lord for many years. I long to see a world where people are not just aware of Christ but truly know Him—living in relationship with Him and fully embracing the purpose He has for them.

Target Audience

In Bangladesh, Muslims constitute about 91% of the population, and Christianity remains a minority faith. Unfortunately, misconceptions about Jesus and Christianity are widespread among the Muslim population. Many of my Muslim friends and neighbors have misconceptions about who Jesus is, and often, they are taught a distorted version of His identity. These misconceptions, along with stereotypes and misunderstandings, make it challenging to have open conversations about faith. But I see a significant opportunity to bridge these gaps and reach out to those who have not yet encountered the truth. I am deeply burdened for the Muslim people of Bangladesh and feel a strong calling to engage with them in love and humility. Through sharing the message of Jesus, I hope to address these misconceptions and promote a more accurate understanding of Christianity. I believe that by breaking down barriers of misunderstanding and showing them the truth of who Jesus really is, I can help lead them to faith in Him. As it is written in Matthew 28:19-20, “Go therefore and make disciples of all nations... teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you,” I believe it is my responsibility to reach out to those who have not yet heard the truth, particularly those in my community who have never had the opportunity to understand who Jesus is.

My Inspiration to Work with God

I have always felt a deep calling to serve God, and my heart has been drawn to His Word. I’ve experienced firsthand the transformative power of His love in my life. From a young age, I’ve had a strong desire to share the truth of the Bible, particularly with those in my community who may have misconceptions about who Jesus is. I believe God’s message is life-changing, and my passion is to be an instrument of His love and truth. My inspiration comes from witnessing the transformation in my parents after they completed their theology program at IGSL. Their journey not only deepened their knowledge but also shaped their character, and I long to experience that same growth. For me, working for God is not just about preaching but about living a life that reflects His love, engaging with others in a way that brings hope, healing, and understanding. I aspire to be a spiritual leader who listens with empathy, speaks with wisdom, and offers counsel that leads to lasting transformation. My calling is rooted in a deep sense of responsibility and gratitude. I want to serve God with all my heart, knowing that my efforts contribute to fulfilling His purpose and helping others find the hope and peace only He can provide. Ultimately, it’s not about seeking my own glory but being faithful to the calling God has placed on my life. I believe He has called me to be worthy of His purpose, to carry His light, and to lead others across the bridge to life in Him.

Why IGSL (Philippines) - The Master of Divinity?

I have chosen IGSL (International Graduate School of Leadership) in the Philippines for my Master of Divinity because it aligns perfectly with my calling and vision. IGSL is not just an institution—it is a training ground for leaders who are deeply rooted in biblical truth and passionate about transforming lives.
One of the primary reasons I am drawn to IGSL is the profound impact it had on my parents. Their time at IGSL deepened their theological understanding, refined their spiritual leadership, and equipped them to serve with wisdom and humility. Seeing their transformation solidified my conviction that IGSL is the right place for me to grow in both knowledge and character. At IGSL, I know that I will receive:
• A strong theological foundation to preach and teach with clarity and conviction.
• Spiritual formation that nurtures my heart and character for long-term ministry.
• Practical leadership training to engage in real-world evangelism and discipleship.
• Cross-cultural exposure that will help me effectively minister in Bangladesh’s diverse religious landscape.
This journey is not just about academic learning—it is about being equipped for a lifetime of impactful ministry. I am excited about the opportunity to learn, grow, and prepare for the unique calling God has placed on my life. My heart’s desire is to be a faithful servant, fully prepared to share the gospel and disciple others for the glory of God.
